Arthur Conan Doyle

The creator of the iconic detective, Sherlock Holmes

Sir Arthur Ignatius Conan Doyle was both a writer and a medical doctor. He was born on May 22, 1859 on Edinburgh, Scotland. Doyle has 9 siblings, and he was the second eldest. He started his Jesuit education on Lancashire, England in 1868. In 1881, He received his bachelor's degree of medicine and surgery qualification. He later got his M.D. in 1885 with his thesis “An Essay upon the Vasomotor Changes in Tabes Dorsalis.”

During his medical education, Conan Doyle was deeply impressed with his professor in observing the most minute detail on the patient's condition. His professor became Conan's inspiration to the titular character series Sherlock Holmes, the first and only consulting detective. The success of the the first novel of Sherlock Holmes prompted Doyle to continue the character's adventures. The Sherlock Holmes stories were considered milestones for crime fiction.

Doyle died on July 7, 1930 at the age of 71 from a heart attack with his last words to his wife: "You are wonderful."
